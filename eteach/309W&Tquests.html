Academic integrity in exams
  • For exams given in class, you must write your answers solely from memory during the exam. No citing or quoting is required. You many not access books or notes of any kind, or collaborate with others, during an in-class exam.
  • For take-home exams (which we will have if an exam must be given remotely):
    • You may use any class materials (assigned texts; notes & other materials posted on Moodle or the linked Google Drive folders) and your own notes from class.
    • No other sources allowed. No collaboration with other people during the exam.
    • The academic integrity instructions will apply just as in out-of-class papers and reading responses. Cite all sources you use and put others’ words in quotation marks.


Info & questions for the early exam (date TBA).

Questions will be posted here before the exam to help you prepare.

The point of this exam is to encourage you to reflect on the material we have covered so far, and consider which elements of it are most important: for jus ad bellum, what the criteria are and what they mean; for realism, pacifism, and just-war theory, what main conclusions different thinkers arrive at and their main reasons for doing so. This matters because these form the background for much of what we will discuss for the rest of the course.

I will ask you to explain views from the areas we have studied. By "explain" I mean: don't attempt to provide every possibly relevant detail. There is not time for that. Instead, your task is to identify and articulate what is most important and distinctive about the views you explain.

On the other hand, it would not be good to try and boil each view down a mere few sentences. Aside from question #1, which is relatively straightforward, these views are too sophisticated to be summarized that briefly. You will have the entire class period, and should use it to demonstrate how well you grasp what we've covered. I recommend ensuring you cover the core of each view you discuss, and then using any remaining time to go back and add detail.
