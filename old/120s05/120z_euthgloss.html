A Quick Glossary of Euthanasia Terms


Killing: Either (a) causing the death of someone who is not already dying, or (b) hastening the death of someone who is already dying. (Note: although people tend to equate killing with murder, you should not assume that killing is immoral. Killing takes place in many contexts where it is considered excusable or even commendable: accident, self-defense, war, executing convicted serial killers, putting sick animals to sleep, etc.)
 
Letting die: Not intervening to save the life of someone who is already dying. (Again, do not assume that letting someone die is either immoral or moral.)
 
Euthanasia (from the Greek for "good death"): Killing another or allowing them to die for their benefit, usually to relieve suffering.
 
Murder: Killing a person in order to harm them, and/or for the killer's benefit. (Legal definitions of murder often stipulate that the killing is "unlawful," to differentiate murder from execution or legal abortion.)
 
Eugenics (from the Greek for "good breeding"): Preventing the continuation or spread of undesirable genetic characteristics (i.e., "cleaning up the gene pool") by preventing those who possess them from marrying or by sterilizing or killing them, in order to benefit society or the race.
 
Active euthanasia (AE): Euthanasia accomplished by killing.
 
Passive euthanasia (PE): Euthanasia accomplished by letting die.
 
Voluntary euthanasia: Euthanasia performed at the request of the one who dies. The request may be by advance directive. It can be active (VAE) or passive (VPE).
 
Non-voluntary euthanasia: Euthanasia performed when the patient's will is unknown because (a) they can't express it now and (b) they haven't left acceptable instructions. It can be active (NAE) or passive (NPE).
 
Involuntary euthanasia: Euthanasia performed against the will of the one who dies. It can be active (IAE) or passive (IPE). IE is so widely considered to be seriously immoral that I do not plan to discuss it in class. (Note: Peter Singer, a prominent ethicist, says that if a patient is competent to decide whether she wants to die but is euthanized without being asked, her death would be involuntary euthanasia also. I tend to agree.)
