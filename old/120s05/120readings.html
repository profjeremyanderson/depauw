Unit IV: The Death Penalty

Readings for the beginning of our study of the death penalty (to be read in addtion to the Supreme Court opinions in Gregg v. Georgia in our book):
1. Read these:
The Case of Stephen Anderson: Two Portraits of Grandmother's Killer
New Doubt Cast on Crime Testing in Houston Cases

2. Skim this: A History of the Death Penalty, from Frontline (a documentary series on PBS).This reading briefly covers the history of the DP in ancient and medieval times, and devotes a lot of attention to the history of the DP in America from colonial times to the present.

3. Methods of execution:
3a. Read this: Methods of Execution in the United States. This page lists which execution methods are used  in each state, and then describes each method in detail. It is on the web site of the the Clark County (Indiana) Prosecutor, who is a retentionist.

3b. Skim this: Did you know stoning and crucifixion were still practiced as of 1999? See Methods of Execution Worldwide, borrowed from Amnesty International, an (among other things) abolitionist organization. This page has two lists: first, a list of nations with the methods of execution they use, and then a list of methods with the number of nations using each.
4. Read this along with Marshall's short piece in our book: Excerpt from Thurgood Marshall's dissenting Opinion in Furman v. Georgia (1972)

Optional reading for the morbidly curious: The Arizona Department of Corrections has a web page on the history of that state's executions. It includes the date, time, and race of those executed and for many of them also includes their last meal requests.

On California's Death Row, the most frequent cause of death is not execution but old age. Here is an interesting article from the Los Angeles Times discussing why death row appeals are so lengthy, executions so rare, and why the death penalty as currently practiced is significantly more expensive than life in prison: Death Row Often Means a Long Life.

Read this LATER, when it's assigned in class: Excerpts from Immanuel Kant's Metaphysics of Morals
