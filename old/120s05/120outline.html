This is a tentative outline for the course. Details and timing are subject to change. Assignments will be announced in class. All readings will be from Social Ethics by Mappes & Zembaty, 6th edition (abbreviated M&Z) unless otherwise noted.
 

Unit I: Introduction to Philosophy in General and Ethics in Particular

Week 1: What philosophy is; approaches to ethics. Read "Evaluating Arguments: Two Helpful Concepts" on the course web site.

Unit II: Abortion

Weeks 2-4: readings from M&Z Chapter 1 and possibly elsewhere.
Week 4 (approximately): Exam #1.

Unit III: Euthanasia & Physician-Assisted Suicide

Weeks 5-7: readings from M&Z Chapter 2 and possibly elsewhere. 
Week 8: Wednesday March 9 is the presentation group & topic selection deadline.
Week 8 (approximately): Midterm Exam (will cover everything but emphasize recent material).

Unit IV: The Death Penalty; and small-group presentations

Weeks 9-11: readings from M&Z Chapter 3 plus supplements on the web and possibly elsewhere. Presentations begin.

Unit V: Animals; and small-group presentations

Weeks 12-14: readings from M&Z Chapter 9 and possibly elsewhere. Presentations continue.

Final exams

The dates and times for final exams are as follows:
For Phil 120-02 (meets 9:30 MW): Friday April 22nd at 9am in our regular classroom.

For Phil 120-03 (meets 11:30 MW): Wednesday April 20th at noon in our regular classroom.

If you take the earlier exam, please be aware that telling others what was on the test is a serious form of cheating. However, doing so may not help others anyway, because any question on one exam may or may not show up on another, so you must prepare for all of the eligible questions.

The final will cover all study questions not on previous exams (i.e., all study questions not struck out on the Study Questions web page), but will emphasize recent material.
